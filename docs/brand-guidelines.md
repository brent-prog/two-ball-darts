# TWO BALL DARTS Brand Guidelines

## Core identity

**Name:** TWO BALL DARTS  
**Tagline:** No gimmes. Just throw.  
**Positioning:** Golf-course scoring for the dartboard. Competitive, simple, pub-friendly, and rules-tight.

## Voice

Direct, competitive, slightly clubhouse. Avoid cheesy drinking-game language. Let the game be fun without explaining the joke.

Preferred phrases:

- No gimmes. Just throw.
- Play the course.
- Count the strokes.
- Low score wins.
- Bulls count for nothing - ever.

Avoid:

- Over-explaining the drinking-game context
- Cartoonish copy
- Fake golf prestige
- AI-sounding rule language

## Colours

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---:|---|
| Deep Green | `#02140f` | Background, page base |
| Fairway Green | `#063927` | Cards, panels, scorecard surfaces |
| Ivory | `#f5e8c6` | Type, outlines, paper-scorecard contrast |
| Golf Gold | `#d0a948` | Borders, dividers, scoring accents |
| Dart Red | `#be1412` | DARTS, warnings, tie-break, CTA |
| Deep Red | `#910f0d` | Button depth, footer bars |

## Logo usage

Use the transparent badge logo on dark green or black backgrounds. Leave clear space around the dart flights and bottom dartboard. Do not crop the logo into the flights or text banner. Do not regenerate or reinterpret the logo.

## Typography

Use condensed, heavy, scoreboard-style typography for headings. Use a sturdy sans-serif for scorecard labels and body copy. The scorecard should feel like a golf-course paper card translated into a dark pub-board interface.

## UI principles

- Scoring table is the hero.
- Scorecards should resemble traditional golf scorecards: holes across the top, player rows, front 9, back 9, total.
- Use visible borders and gridlines.
- Make tap targets large enough for game-night mobile use.
- Rule clarification must be deterministic and official, not creative.
